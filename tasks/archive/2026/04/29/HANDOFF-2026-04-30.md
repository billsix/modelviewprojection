### 5. Per-demo `.vert` pairing rule (CRITICAL — see feedback memory)
During Phase 2 verification Bill found the perspective frustum animation
became "discrete" instead of smooth. Root cause: I'd switched the frustum
and cube to `axis.vert + triangle.frag`, but in the perspective demo
`axis.vert` has a *different* projection-animation curve from
`frustum.vert`:
- `axis.vert` uses `max(1.0, ...)` + `if (time<X) m=identity` → SNAPS
- `frustum.vert` uses `min((time-X)/5, 1)` + interpolated `xVal` → SMOOTH
- `cube.vert` has NO `project()` at all (cube is the static NDC reference)

Fix: each pipeline now uses **the vert shader of its own name**:
- `axis_program` → `axis.vert + triangle.frag`
- `cube_program` → `cube.vert + triangle.frag`
- `frustum_program` → `frustum.vert + triangle.frag` (ortho) or
  `frustum.vert + frustum.frag + frustum.geom` (perspective — see #6)
- `ground_program` → `ground.vert + triangle.frag`

### 6. Perspective frustum reverted to screen-space-thickness ribbons
Bill noticed that during the projection squash animation, the back edges of
the frustum looked stippled. Cause: frustum's `project()` scales back-edge
X/Y by `near_z / far_z ≈ 0.04` (25× compression). Cylinders with
world-space radius 0.05 go sub-pixel during this squash; the rasterizer
can't fill the triangles solidly, creating visible gaps.

The fix was to revert the perspective frustum specifically back to
`frustum.vert + frustum.frag + frustum.geom`. The geom shader expands each
line into a screen-space-thickness quad (`u_thickness` pixels wide, depth-
independent), so the back edges stay solid no matter how aggressive the
squash. See `/mvp/tasks/archive/2026/04/29/notes-screen-space-thickness-geom-shader.md` for
the credit / explanation.

The cube and axes in the perspective demo stay as cylinders; only the
frustum needed the screen-space treatment because only the frustum
animates through the squash. The ortho frustum is still cylinders (its
squash is much milder, `2/15 ≈ 0.13`, and didn't trigger the issue).
